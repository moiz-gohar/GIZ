About Us

The Climate Resilient Infrastructure Challenge Fund aims to strengthen climate resilience in Pakistan by driving mission-focused innovation through infrastructure projects in Khyber Pakhtunkhwa (KP), Punjab, and Islamabad Capital Territory (ICT). Targeting critical vulnerabilities such as flood-prone urban centers, water-scarce agricultural areas, and peri-urban zones facing extreme weather, the fund addresses pressing climate challenges while aligning with Pakistan's National Adaptation Plan (NAP) to deliver adaptive, high-impact solutions.

  • Who can apply for the Challenge Fund?

    The Challenge Fund is open to Small and Medium Enterprises (SMEs), Non-Governmental Organizations (NGOs), startups, social enterprises, and informal groups, provided informal groups partner with an eligible organization.

  • Are groups like women's collectives or youth initiatives eligible to apply?

    Yes, informal groups, such as women's collectives or youth-led initiatives, may apply by partnering with an eligible organization like an NGO, which would act as the primary applicant.
